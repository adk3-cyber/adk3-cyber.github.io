**Assignment overview**

This assignment asked us to create a little stuffed animal with a circuit which turns on lights on the front of the stuffed thing. This circuit was the first time when we used conductive thread to create the circuit within the plushy. In order to do this, we first had to create a blueprint on paper and then use the alligator clips with wires to make sure the design worked before copying that onto the felt which is cut to be the same shape as the paper prototype. Placement of the leds, the battery, and the switch/button were crucial to this stage. To hide the battery, we had to sew it to the inside of the plushy, then we had to place the switch and button between the battery and leds so that only when the switch is on and the button pushed will the lights turn on. Once all that was done, we had to stitch the plushy together and stuff it frowith paper towels. 

**Tip or trick**
When you're trying this for the first time make sure that you really mae sure that you don't split or tangle the conductive thread because it's really hard to disentangle and really easy to tangle.
